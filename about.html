Amanda Dahl

Amanda Dahl is a technology and policy leader whose work sits at the intersection of digital infrastructure, emerging technology governance and public policy. Over a career spanning more than thirty years she has helped build and guide complex technology systems across government, global technology companies and public institutions, with a focus on ensuring that innovation delivers public value while maintaining security, accountability and trust.

Amanda currently serves as Director of Product and Strategy at The Crown Estate, where she leads enterprise digital strategy across product development, data infrastructure, cybersecurity and innovation. She directs a technology organisation of more than eighty specialists and oversees a £30 million annual technology investment supporting a £19 billion national estate. Her work includes establishing enterprise AI governance frameworks and directing marine data platforms that support spatial planning across the UK seabed, balancing priorities including offshore wind, energy infrastructure and environmental protection.

Previously Amanda was Deputy Director for Products and Services at the UK Government Digital Service within the Cabinet Office. There she directed multidisciplinary teams responsible for seven national digital platforms serving government departments across the UK. These included GOV.UK Pay, which processes billions of pounds in public payments, and GOV.UK Notify, which has delivered over ten billion communications to citizens. She also led the launch of the UK’s Emergency Alerts system, a national resilience capability developed in partnership with COBR and major telecommunications providers.

Before returning to the UK Amanda served in the United States Digital Service at the White House, where she worked on major federal service transformation and crisis response efforts. During the national infant formula shortage she led an interagency team working with the White House Chief of Staff’s office, the National Economic Council and the Domestic Policy Council to analyse supply chain risks and support emergency policy responses.

Amanda’s earlier career includes senior product and platform leadership roles at Sony Interactive Entertainment (PlayStation) and the BBC. At PlayStation she transformed a global digital supply chain serving thousands of game developers and managing billions of dollars in digital products. At the BBC she built the myBBC analytics platform that enabled hundreds of editorial staff to use real-time audience insight to shape digital services reaching millions of users.

Alongside operational leadership Amanda has remained deeply engaged with technology policy and governance. She has represented the United Kingdom internationally as thematic lead for Artificial Intelligence and Digital Democracy at the OECD, working with governments across Europe, Asia and the Americas on emerging technology governance. She is also a Policy Fellow at the University of Cambridge Centre for Science and Policy.

Amanda is currently completing a Master’s degree in AI Ethics and Society at the University of Cambridge, where her research examines how AI prioritisation systems affect human agency in public welfare contexts. Her work explores how institutions can design and govern complex digital systems in ways that maintain democratic accountability while enabling innovation.
